# Post-Go-Live Feature Wave

This phase starts only after the current release-candidate gate reaches GO quality.

## Goal

Run the app with 15 distinct personas to identify missing features, weak content, weak emotional payoff, retention gaps, and MRR opportunities. The output is not just bug QA; it is a product-expansion pass focused on WOW effect, user bonding, personalization, and monetization.

## Required Pass

- Build 15 personas that cover likely Reality Check users: different relationship contexts, emotional states, willingness to pay, notification tolerance, privacy sensitivity, and sharing behavior.
- Play through the full app for each persona: onboarding, check creation, voice/text moment input, free verdict, prompts, paywall, locked state, unlock, daily, move-on, settings, sharing, empty/returning states.
- Record what each persona expected, where the app felt generic, what content felt repetitive, and where the app missed a chance to feel personally specific.
- Identify missing features and content surfaces from those walkthroughs.
- Evaluate each idea for WOW effect, retention/user-bonding, shareability, LLM-personalization leverage, implementation cost, safety risk, and MRR potential.
- Turn the best opportunities into a prioritized product/UX plan.

## LLM Direction

The LLM layer may play a stronger role after go-live if it increases real personalization. It must still follow the existing safety rules: deterministic score, grounded receipts, no invented events, no crisis-roast path, no client-side API key, and no wound-backward copy.

## Output

- Persona matrix with all 15 walkthrough findings.
- Feature/content gap analysis.
- WOW/UX/user-bonding review.
- MRR opportunity analysis.
- Prioritized creative product roadmap for the next wave.
